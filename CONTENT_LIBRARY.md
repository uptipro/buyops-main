## FAQ (id: faq)

**Section title**

- “Frequently Asked Questions”

**Section subtitle**

- “Everything you need to know about fractional real estate investment”

**FAQ items**

1. Q: “What is fractional real estate ownership?”
   - A: “Fractional ownership means you can buy a portion (fraction) of a property instead of the whole thing. Each fraction represents actual ownership, not just shares or percentages. You receive a unique registration code (PR-HRL-####) and a digital certificate proving your ownership.”
2. Q: “How does the payment process work?”
   - A: “We maintain a strict zero-cash policy. All transactions happen through secure payment links generated by sales agents. Once you confirm your purchase, the payment is processed through bank-grade encrypted gateways, and your ownership is instantly recorded in the system.”
3. Q: “What are the three BuyOps apps?”
   - A: “BuyOps Admin is the governance layer for enterprise management. BuyOps Sales is the velocity layer for agents to manage leads and close deals. BuyOps Investor is the liquidity layer where you can browse properties, buy fractions, and track your portfolio.”
4. Q: “Is my investment secure?”
   - A: “Absolutely. Every property undergoes legal title verification, property valuation, and compliance certification before listing. All transactions are digitally recorded with blockchain-backed verification. Your digital certificate and unique PR-HRL code provide immutable proof of ownership.”
5. Q: “What happens after I purchase a fraction?”
   - A: “Upon successful payment, the system auto-generates your unique Registration Code (PR-HRL-####) and issues a Digital Deed. You gain immediate access to detailed property information, projected returns, and can track your investment through the Investor dashboard.”
6. Q: “What fees are involved?”
   - A: “We believe in transparency. All fees are clearly displayed before purchase, including platform fees, transaction fees, and any property management costs. There are no hidden charges. Everything is priced and settled in Nigerian Naira (₦).”
